Lythra
As the southeastern subregion of Gaia, Lythra is a melting pot of many cultures. With Promethea Bay as the main point of entry into the region, Pyrinas - both Gaia's and Lythra's capital - has become one of the most populated cities in the region. In Chestasio, Lythra experiences mild and temperate winter-like weather. Snowy days are the norm, but rain is not uncommon, though the rains tend to freeze over once the temperatures dip down below freezing.

Callitris
On the northwestern shore of Gaia sits Callitris, a land coated in snow and ice. Though often barren and desolate, the few who hunger for knowledge, and seek adventure in the permafrosted plains don't hesitate to call the region home. In Chestasio, Callitris is starting to cool off before the true chill of Earnia. The temperatures are still manageable though the air is noticeably drier and the wind is starting to pick up. The Aural Lights are strongest in Chestasio.

Parrya
Surrounded by mountains and filled with dunes, Parrya is the northeastern subregion of Gaia. Travelers and locals flock to the oasis that's come to be known as Mas Erimos, Gaia's newest metropolis: the city of glitz and glamour, built out of glass. The air is always dry in Parrya, but during Chestasio the region gets a reprieve from the oppressive heat of the desert. The nights are cooler and a little bit longer, and it's not uncommon to experience frosty mornings.

Ilex
The beautiful southwestern coast of Gaia is home to the ever-bountiful Ilex, teeming with flora, fauna and human-life everywhere you look. Sandy beaches, lush rainforests and historical attractions make Ilex a place of both rest, and exploration. While other regions prepare for cooler temperatures, Ilex remains constant. Chestasio will come and go without any major changes to the rainforest's climate, with the temperatures still comfortably warm and the air still wet and humid.

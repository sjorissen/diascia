Lythra

As the southeastern subregion of Gaia, Lythra is a melting pot of many cultures. With Promethea Bay as the main point of entry into the region, Pyrinas - both Gaia's and Lythra's capital - has become one of the most populated cities in the region.

In Chestasio, Lythra experiences mild and temperate winter-like weather. Snowy days are the norm, but rain is not uncommon, though the rains tend to freeze over once the temperatures dip down below freezing.

Pyrinas

The gate to Gaia — the port town of Pyrinas is the region’s oldest and largest city. It sits in the cradle of Promethea Bay, and is often viewed as the heart of the region. Like most cities that predate their states, the center is made up of old masonry work, with old buildings that now house modern governments, offices, and upscale housing. It's also the birthplace of Gaia's main bullet train, Trenochia, which has helped secure Pyrinas’ popularity throughout the years, bringing in and connecting residents all across Gaia. Today, the city is a hotspot for Strategos, both for the accessibility of travel, and the abundance of events hosted for them.

Lythra’s clement weather has nurtured bucolic, rolling hills with rich native grasses, old-world trees and small brooks. As you get close to Ilex the flora and fauna equally begin to grow thicker.

Promethea Bay

Promethea Bay has a broad, extensive beach that gently slopes into ocean waters; a man-made channel cleft through the seabed makes for a forgiving entry to Pyrinas’s harbor. Due to this nature, it’s a popular beach destination with hundreds of meters of walkable, shallow water.

However, don't let the serenity of the surroundings fool you, either side of the bay is peppered with small shipwrecks, and dangerous, unexplored mini-archipelagos.

Korenth

Sitting far northwest, and on the border between Lythra and Callitris, Korenth was carved into the Gaian Mountains, a little north of the Notrasima river, over a hundred years ago in the name of gem and precious metal mining.Today, it's home to many famous jewellers and goldsmiths.

It boasts a high-speed boating connection to its Ilexian sister-city of Elaia, and the recently-built Korae Schisme bullet train line, to connect it to its Parryan sister-city of Surae.

Elsewhere

Despite some beliefs, Gaia is not the centre of the PokéWorld, and there exists many regions, cities and towns outside of its borders that offer a wealth of experiences and memories for those willing to venture to them. Or perhaps, back to them.
